Context

Food delivery systems and restaurant order are becoming convenient in the 21st century as mobile technology is advancing.


The way we create food and the purpose of delivering food are about to change


When it comes to ordering food and looking for food, how often do people actually want to eat the food from the restaurant? If people eat/order food from restaurants often, why is it that people order food instead of cooking at home? Do people actually know how to cook food? For people who want to cook food at home, can people find the right resources and information on the recipe? Is there a way to become better at cooking and share with other people about your recipe?

User Groups & Stakeholders

Today, there are food delivery services such as UberEats, GrubHub, and DoorDash that delivers restaurant meals from the restaurant to people’s home. With the food delivery services, users do not have to leave their home or call the restaurants for food delivery details. The current food delivery services’ user group is limited to people who are wanting to order meals from the restaurants, University dining halls and some food truck meals. With home cooked meal sharing services, the user not only gets their food delivered instantly, but also gets to learn each other's recipe and immerse themselves into the creative cooking world. For instance, a busy 30 year old man who never has ingredients at home due to busy day at work can order ingredients from people around him and learn about special recipes from other people around the area.


Perhaps it is crucial for designers, engineers, and professional home cooks to collaborate and shape the future of cooking and social connection.

User Interviews

Overview:
I was able to bring 10 participants (Age ranging from 21 - 55) to Bloomington Wells Library to conduct an interview regarding the food delivery services such as the UberEats, DoorDash and Grubhub. The main focus of the interview was to gather feedback on people’s experience in cooking at home, preparing ingredients for food, current food delivery services and technology. Before the user interview process, I analysed and prepared all the appropriate questions. In the beginning of this user interview process, I asked 6 participants’ permission for an interview and photography of the user interview process. This method helped gather rich data and feedback in people’s experience with how people cook food at home, how people prepare food at home, food delivery service, food restriction management, and mobile app interaction.

Questions:

How often do you find yourself cooking at home?

When do you find yourself cooking at home?

What do you do to get yourself an ingredient to prepare your meal?

How often do you find yourself ordering food from a restaurant and what are the possible price ranges?

If cooking at home distracts you from other events, what is it and what do you think is the main problem?

How do you deal with finances when purchasing food from a restaurant?

What is the ideal portion size for your average meal? And have the portion size ever changed?

Have you tried cooking new food at home and is your cooking process successful?

Have you ever felt frustrated or confused with the food delivery services?

What is your least favourite thing about the food delivery service?

Key Findings:

I don’t have time to cook at home at all during the daytime or even morning time because I have to get ready to go to work

Getting up at 7:00 am for work is rough so I just skip breakfast

Usually I go to grocery shopping at night but the main problem is that there’s always a time when I forget to buy certain things

I usually cook at home during the weekends when I don’t have plans

Me and my wife usually drive to the local Kroger to get the ingredients I need for cooking

I’m either at work or at school taking courses during the day so when I come home, I become tired and sometimes don’t have enough energy to go grocery shopping

I usually eat cereals, milk and egg in the morning and I would have to rush through the breakfast

I love cooking at home during the weekend if I have the ingredients and resources that I need

I don’t understand why DoorDash charge delivery fees sometimes and sometimes don’t

When I ordered french fries from Joella’s Chicken, the delivery driver took 45 minutes to arrive and the fries came cold. I try not to order fries again.

Design Concept and Sketches

Overview:

From the user overview, I learned that there are 3 key factors that distracts user from cooking at home. The three main factor were the accessibility, timing and finance. Many people pointed out the accessibility for the distance between their residence and nearest grocery stores. Furthermore, people pointed out the lack of timing they have for personal cooking at home. For the mock-up initial design, the name of the website is named the Bloomington Go Food.

High Fidelity Design Descriptions

Figure 1: Home page of the HomeGo Food website. When user visits the home page for the first time, user is directed to the sign up button, when user is already logged into the account, user's name is displayed and ready to explore different pages within the website. Within the home page, user is able to view the most trending food and user is able to read the introduction of the website.

Figure 2: Sign up page for the user. In the sign up page, user fills in important credentials for their account (i.e First name, Last name, DOB, and etc). In addition to user credential, there are food preference selection and dietary restriction options where user is able to modify their preferences. This is done so that when the user purchases a meal or ingredients from the website, user is able to pick food without having to worry about the ingredients in the meal.

Figure 3: Foody Media (Social media for food sharing). Within the Foody-Media feature in the website, user is able to home cooked meal created by other users. When user is interested in purchasing or knowing more about the ingredients of other person's meal, user may simply click on person's profile and make connections/contact for further options such as home cooked meal delivery and/or person's special recipe training.

Figure 4: Explore page of the website. User is able to view trending food created by near-by users. In addition, the website generates a cultural food of the day and displays a new food options for the user.

Figure 5: Profile page when user clicks on people's profile. User is able to click follow to make connection with other users. When user clicks on purchase, user is able to make purchaseperson's meals from person's profile. In the profile page, user is able to view all the meals that other user has created.

Figure 6: Once user clicks on the food, user is directed for either meal purchase from the person or meal recipe and ingredient tab. Similar to Amazon's purchase concept, once user click on "Add to the cart" option, user's meal is added to the cart and user can press proceed to payment for secure payments.

Figure 7: HomeGoFood market, an ingredient market for this website. As discussed in the key findings section, some users are having difficulty finding time for grocery shopping for ingredients. The grocery shopping for produces, ingredients and spicies are added to this website so that user may purchase their ingredients without having to drive/walk to the grocery store. This helps users on time saving and energy saving

Reflecting on the Project

This project had major changes throughout the project. Initially, this project was targeted toward Indiana University students only for the testing purposes. Therefore, I focused the market plans toward IU students only. Through the user interviews, I learned that many people including both students and non students are facing challenges in home cooking due to accessibility, timing and financial reasons. Although systems like UberEats, DoorDash and GrubHub offers a restaurant delivery, often times people get frustrated on long delivery times, ingredients that they do not want on the food and financial outcomes. In order to make people creative and connected, making food at home, cooking their food without having to worry about accessibilty and finance are important factors and solutions. Throughout the project, I learned how to apply user interview to make a better product for the user. I learned how to analyze current product (i.e GrubHub, DoorDash and UberEats) and pain point on user's story with the current technology.
