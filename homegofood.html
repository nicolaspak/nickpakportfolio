HOME RESUME

HomeGoFood Delivery

Designed for people with interest in cooking, solving challenges in food waste and idea to reduce the cost of food for the community.

OVERVIEW

About the project

Problem: Throughout the past couple of years, many people started using mobile app such as UberEats, DoorDash, and GrubHub for food delivery. However, these mobile delivery can be costly, and sometimes difficult for many young adults to financially afford them. Many adults continue to face challenges in accessing resources to food ingredients, recipes, and affordability.

Solution: The solution proposed is a social media type web application that allows people to learn, purchase food, purchase ingredients, and share food between other people in the community. User can search for ingredients, search for people, and buy/purchase food and ingredients from other people.

Duration: 2 Months

My role: I worked individually on this entire project from the research to final design concept. I conducted a stakeholder research, interviews, sketching, low fidelity design, and visual design mockup using Adobe XD.

CHALLENGE

Context: When it comes to ordering food and looking for food, how often do people actually want to eat the food from the restaurant? If people eat/order food from restaurants often, why is it that people order food instead of cooking at home? For people who want to cook food at home, can people find the right resources and information on the recipe? Is there a way to become better at cooking and share with other people about your recipe?

RESEARCH

Identifying the user needs

For this project, I conducted an user interview to 5 people. The main focus of the interview was to gather feedback on people’s experience in cooking at home, preparing ingredients for food, current food delivery services and technology. Before the user interview process, I analysed and prepared all the appropriate questions. This method helped gather rich data and feedback in people’s experience with how people cook food at home, how people prepare food at home, food delivery service, food restriction management, and mobile app interaction. I wanted answers to questions such as:

  • What do you do to get yourself an ingredient to prepare your meal?
  • When do you find yourself cooking at home?
  • How do you deal with finances when purchasing food from a restaurant?
  • How often do you find yourself cooking at home?

Interviews

I conducted an user interview to 5 people ranging from age 19 to age 55. Some interviewers are in college, and some interviewers are adults (young and old). Most people stated that they face challenges in timing and affordability of food ingredients/cooking (i.e finding time to go to the store when they are busy). Some people stated that ordering food at a restaurant is expensive. More details will be covered in the research findings section.

RESEARCH FINDINGS

Highlighting the Frustrations

I pulled together the key results from the interview research I conducted. After the interview, I took away some key pain points and took away 3 major problems from people's habit with cooking or accessing food.

Financial Issue

For many young adults and students who are in a tight budget, getting food delivered and purchasing food ingredients can be pricey. This is a key issue because young adults are either at school focusing on academics or most young adults are earning base income with rising food costs.

Timing Issue

Many people stated that they skip breakfast or some form of meal at least in average day. This is because of their busy schedule and work. Both young adults and old adults have different meal time each day mainly due to the busyness of their daily life.

Accessibility Issue

Some people has stated that restaurants, grocery stores, and convenient stores are too far away from their home or office. For some people, they don't have a car to drive to get food or food ingredients. Accessibility can be a big issue when dealing with food/meals.

Exploring Ideas and Preliminary Sketches

DESIGN DECISION

Understanding the Constraints, Requirements, and the Feature

After the user research, and preliminary sketches, I highlighted the important constraints features, and requirements that I should be aware of for the design desicions.

Constraints

  • Navigation links should be easy to understand
  • Some people have different preferences in types of food
  • Some users eat different amount of meal per day
  • There are users who would want to learn how to cook
  • Need to consider the financial aspect for the users
  • Users need friends and people who are influential
  • Users need to be encouraged not discouraged
  • Users may not want to explore same types of food all the time

Requirements

  • Implement food preferences for the user for dietary restrictions
  • Safety background check and user agreement before sharing cooked meal to others (i.e not inserting poison, using expired food)
  • Age restrictions of 18+ for alcohol contained food
  • Community guideline agreement and secure payment feature to protect fraud (i.e not paying for food)
  • Safe but accessible food ingredients for all

Features

  • User signup, login, and profile settings that includes editable food preferences
  • Social media features (follow, and chat) to learn different food recipes
  • Secure payment page for secure payment of products available in this website
  • Search bar, and explore tab that allows users to explore food from different regions of the world
  • Like & Share option for public awareness

VISUAL DESIGN

High Fidelity Prototyping

I started designing a high fidelity prototype after understanding the user requirements, features, and constraints. Details on features, screens, and services are depicted below.

Sign up, login, and landing page

The landing page includes users to learn about what this platform is about. Although the landing page does not seem minimalistic, the landing page gives convincing information on what users will gain from the media. The media is titled HomeGoFood, users can sign up using their credentials and select their preferences and restrictions before moving forward.

Foody-Media, and Explore page

As mentioned in preliminary sketches and design decisions, users need friends where they can be influenced and encouraged by others. In order to solve networking challenges, I created a Foody-Media social media page where users can interact between other cookers, visit other profiles, follow others who inspired them, and learn to cook. Explore options give users to explore food from different regions around the world. With implementation of this page, users will be able to be inspired by unique cultures around the world and learn more about global culinary history & trends.

Each users are also able to earn points (aka chef points) when they cook foods, and share with others in the community. Regardless of food being tasty or not, users will earn points for learning how to cook, finishing their cooking experience, and other users will be able to view people's chef points for inspiration. Users can always purchase food from others and earn points through frequently purchasing.

Recipe & Secure Food Ordering

When users visit people's profile, users can interact others by liking or sharing other people's food update. More importantly, users can directly communicate with other people, learn other people's recipe, and even purchase food from other people. When purchasing food from others, the payment is conducted securely by entering user's card information on their own profile. When learning recipes from others, users can select any option that person is specialized in and there will be a step by step guidelines for cooking.

HomeGo Food Ingredient Shopping

From the research findings, one of the challenges many users faced were the accessibility and timing of grocery shopping. In order to make cooking more convenient for users, I implemented a HomeGoFood ingredient delivery shopping that brings fresh produce, and ingredients to user's home.

CONCLUSION

Reflection & Next Steps

This project was my first UX/HCI project I've ever conducted. This was my first time conducting user research using user interview and experiencing UX projects in an advanced level. Overall, I have learned many new skills as a UX designer. In user research interview, I learned how to gather and analyze data by framing the key issues that users are facing. Then, I learned how to examine the key problems that users are facing in daily life in eating habits and learned how to apply those key takings to preliminary sketches. I was able to generate concepts and ideas from the key issues highlighted, which lead me to the low fidelity drawings of UI components, and come to the design decisions for the users.


Key Takeaways

I was given a feedback that some of the website visuals and layouts are not following the current design standards. In order to determine the issue of the design and understand the feedback, I went back to the final prototype and reflected upon the design choices I made. I learned that some of the typography choices made hard for some users to read and learned that color choices were not up to current design standards. In order to fix these issues and to learn better, I searched on Google, 2019 UX trends and took away some great advices, and visual inspiration from different resources. I started accepting where I am as a UX Designer and my skills, and decided to aim toward learning more about UX principles, and visual UI trends from inspirational resources.
