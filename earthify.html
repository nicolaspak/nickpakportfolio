HOME RESUME

Earthify Mobile App

Discovering new ways to reduce food waste, and action toward climate change.

OVERVIEW

About the project

Problem: Food waste is becoming a big issue for world's climate change and its becoming a big global challenge. Some people end up with abundant food but leave a lots of food waste, while some people in the world are starving. Even today, many people throw away food items and harmful waste without thoughts and consideration. This project examines people's behavior with food waste, and recycling practices.

Solution: The solution proposed is a mobile application that helps users to reduce waste and properly manage their waste. When users have left over food item or utensil waste that can be reused, users are able to share the left over items to the people around them and work benefit from managing their waste properly (potential earnings).

Duration: 2 Months

My role: This project was an individual project where I got to conduct competitive analysis, user interviews, behavioral studies, literature review, and create a visual design prototype. Due to COVID-19 pandemic, this project was impacted by program schedule and many changes were reimplemented.

CONTEXT

The Challenge

Identify people's waste management experience, and insights and design a solution to help people to have better recycling, and waste management habits such as not throwing away left over fresh produces and fruits. Long term goal is to make people use this particular product and help to make an impact for environmental changes.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Examining the latest technology

In order to identify the latest effort toward waste management and learn more about the current technologies that are invented to fight against the food waste, I conducted a competitive analysis to understand some of the latest technological methods that are used to manage wastes. I researched 4 different products for this competitive analysis and took away some great key points on some of the latest product funtionality.

OnePlus Systems

Ultrasonic waste bin sensor that notifies users how full the container is, providing detailed analytics on the load of waste and providing details on composting practices. The container keeps in track of all the contents in the bin and helps users to optimize the amount of waste in the container. The benefits include:

  • Appropriate replacing of the bin
  • Fuel cost of the waste management truck

ECube Labs

The pickup bin uses solar power energy to compress all the waste that is stored in the bin. In addition, the pickup bin allows users to place more waste by 80% in volume. The benefits include:

  • No hand uses for compression
  • Reduces time by solar energy compressor

ecoATM Gazelle Kiosk

Mainly targeted toward e-waste management. The key issue with e-waste is that most people tend to throw away e-waste in a random place such as composting and recycling. Located in 2800 different locations throughout the United States, this product is designed as an ATM system where users can store their used mobile phones, computer devices and tablets in the ATM like kiosks.

  • Mobile phone and tablet categorisation
  • Users earn money for the e-waste compostment
  • Safe replacement and e-waste compostment
  • Other users are able to earn electronic devices

Bin-E

This product is able to store any waste onto the waste management machine. When the waste is placed on the machine, the machine detects the shape and the colour of the waste. It scans the waste and the waste is categorized without the user having to think about where the waste belongs.

  • Reducing CO2 emissions
  • Users save time when in a hurry

BEHAVIORIAL MAPPING

Observations on people's waste management habits

Now that I closely researched competitive market and understood the latest techology people utilize to manage waste, I wanted to idenfity and see if people would have a better understanding and knowledge in managing waste. As a result, I conducted an Observational Behavioral Mapping method to closely observe people's waste management patterns, and understand people's habits in a daily life environment. The overall goal for this research method was to gain knowledge and understanding in how people manage their waste and understand the pattern in 2020.

Settings, locations, and notes

The behavioral mapping and observation session took in place at cafeteria in Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engieering at Indiana University, from 12:40 pm to 1:40 pm during a busy lunch hour. During the observation, I had a paper and pen and started taking detailed notes from the observation. Throughout the 1 hour period of time, I have witnessed many people rushing to places, and throwing away plastic cups into the landfill bin, and placing untouched (fresh) food waste into the recycling bin. In order to capture and understand people's movements, I drew out a diagram of waste bin, recycling bin, people's movement, and objects into a Adobe Illustrator diagram.

Key Findings

From this observation and behavior mapping, I learned that people’s behavior differs based on the situation they are in. Starting with the list of items thrown away, about 75% of people (which is the majority of the items thrown away) threw away paper wraps and paper receipts received from the Luddy Hall cafe. Additionally, people threw away their wooden plates and compostable utensils into any bin (without seperating). Lastly, many people threw away paper cups from Luddy cafe, which was about 10% of the waste. I learned that many people in the school environment are in a busy situation where many students and faculties do not spend time categorizing their waste. Many students ignored the fact that there are composting and recycling bins separately and many people placed their trash in any bins they saw on the way.

LITERATURE REVIEW

Ideas behind literature

From the project development process, I had a chance to discuss the data and results gathered from observational studies and competitive market analysis. I wanted to discover a deeper analysis on the current existing technology and how the technology is benefiting the society today. As a result, I conducted a literature review research analysis on two readings and produced a paper summary for each report. Throughout reading, I discovered many user-friendly features from the current application services. The main focus was to find out how current technology is benefiting the community around them not just for one person individually.

​Social Recipe Recommendation to Reduce Food Waste

The product mentioned in this reading is a software application (EUPHORIA) GPS tracked technology where leftover recipes in the refrigerator can be shared with other people around the community. This application provides both qualitative and quantitative data that allows other users to view different percentage levels for each recipe and request the ones that other users need. This reading not only focuses on the technical features that this app offers but also explores the behaviour and explores the user’s emotion when using this device. Making a connection to my thought process and thinking, I previously worked on the UX project where users can create a special meal that they specialize in and share it with other users who are feeling hungry or in need of food.

​The Grumpy Bin: Reducing Food Waste Through Playful Social Interaction

The product mentioned in this reading is called the Grumpy Bin, which allows young students and young adults to get into the better recycling and food waste managing habits. This particular system is designed for students who are facing difficulties and challenges in properly managing food waste or recycling. Additionally, this product is designed for people who live with other roommates and not individually. In the reading paper summary documentation, I discovered how this particular product can bring in team effort to minimise any waste people produce. During this case, I was able to learn and reflect about the recycling culture of the United States and how this product can help the University and local towns to reduce their time managing food waste that is caused by the young students and young adults.

USER INTERVIEW

Virtual user interview

To closely visualize and understand user's habit with daily waste management practices, I conducted an interview with one person virtually. During this interview session, I asked questions regarding daily waste management habits, waste management difficulties, and methods to manage waste. Most questions were qualitative with open ended questions. The questions included:

  • On a scale of 1-10, what would you rate yourself in composting materials in a proper way?
  • Where do you find yourself (In what environment) difficult to compost?
  • In a home environment. Do you find yourself composting well?
  • Do you use any sort of technology or a website to compost?
  • There is a company called Green Camino where they pick up your composts. Would you use that service?

Overall key findings

From the observation and research I conducted for this process, I had a chance to observe different technologies from other markets, compare, reflect and brainstorm on the new potential product to help users to get into the better waste management practices. Along with the important idea generation on technical features, I have specifically learned that sensor technology would play a huge role in the technical component. While I was conducting research through the literature reviews, virtual interview and competitive market analysis, I had a chance to observe the on-going news with the coronavirus pandemic outbreak and see major changes in our daily waste management habits. VISUAL DESIGN & PROPOSAL

Design Decision

From the research and synthesis, I was able to identify user's pattern and understand user's habits in waste management. I learned that the major issues with improper waste management practice were the factor of timing and accessibility. When people are in a rush and do not have time to handle the junk, I found out that people sometimes place food compost waste in the recycling bin and throw away recyclable items in the compost bin without further thinking. By incorporating the latest product concept I discovered in the competitive analysis and literature review, and to connect with user's frustration, I decided to design an app that allows users to take a picture of their fresh left over items (food waste, utensils waste, and etc) and share those items to nearby people. In order for users to have go through convenient, reliable, and safe item sharing process, I designed the scan image processing feature for food quality testing, with safe login, signup, and payment feature.

Easy login and signup features

In order to attract users to use our eco-friendly product, I added an introduction what the application helps user accomplish. Then, the get started button lets users to either login, create their account, or signup.

Home menu for options, and image scanning

To make clear navigations for different services offered, there are 4 boxes with clear descriptions about each functionality. Users can shop, share food, check their progress on waste management, and empty the bin that is tied to this app. Image processing allows users to scan any left over item for quality check and approves when the item is in good condition.

Quality check rejection, item sharing, and shop menu

For people's safety and for the sanitation purposes, the image scanner may reject the item to share if the scanner spots mold, or unsanitary spot on the food. Users have option to share other left overs if they have any, and if the item quality check is successfully approved, then users can enter in their information on the quanitity they are selling and customize prices.

Shop category and details

Users have options to purchase other people's left over/unused item from other people. Shop option allows users to select a category, select any option from crowdsourcing formatted display, and order/purchase the item from the users who shared the item.

My progress and bin management

The goal for this feature is to have users get in a habit of proper waste handling, management, and recycling practices. In order to inspire and encourage users to this effort, I designed a my progress feature that keeps in track of waste handling and management. Bin management works with the bin that is associated/registered with the application. Using the Arduino sensoring attached to the bin, users can click compress button and compress all the items that are in the waste bin. Similar concept outlined in ECube-Labs competitive analysis, this will help to reduce constant waste dumping.
