Context

Over the past 3 decades, the way we communicate, interact and respond to daily reminders, messages and tasks have changed drastically. In the 1990s, we responded to our communication by beeping pagers, then it moved onto answering text messages using hand motion/gestures. Since the first introduction of the smartphone, people now read and tap on the flat screen to respond to the notifications and reminders. Technologies are continuing to evolve and there are new technological devices coming up every year with different features/functionality.


However, 3154 people have been killed and 424,000 people have been injured in 2013 by smartphone notification distractions while driving.


It is now time to reduce the injury/fatality caused by smartphone distraction, and with the new introduction and new practices with VR technology, there needs to be a better way to view personal notification and avoid distractions.

Contextual Inquiry

Focus Groups & User Interviews

Initially as a group, we wanted to hear the user's voice and thoughts on the current notification systems. There are many different devices users use, such as iPhone, Android, Android tablets, iPads, iPod Touch, and etc.


Summary

Our topic space of push notification management led us to decide on a focus group targeting busy individuals with many commitments, who may benefit from the organization of their notifications. We also thought to extend it to include avid social media users, but our main focus were individuals with a busy schedule. Each of us brought one person that met the criteria of being a busy individual who has intense schedules that involve managing organizations, school, jobs, and etc. If we couldn't find someone to fit that criteria, then we would have someone who engaged with social media regularly brought in as a replacement, so we could hear their thoughts and perspective on the matter. Each of us happened to know students from IU who were either involved in many different activities on campus or consistently used social media. The focus group took place on Tuesday, October 29th in a secluded conference room at Nicolas's apartment complex at 9:30 at night. The meeting took approximately 45 minutes and we joined our subjects at the table to ask them questions and observe them discussing these topics.

Mind Mapping & Card Sorting

Mind Mapping

Mind Map Objectives

Our group used a 3 x 5 foot white board to draw and list out all ideas/concepts with VR technology and notification management. Each member in our group came in with a general unique concept with VR glass technology and notification management. Mind mapping was a great way to visualise different aspects of the project and build new ideas upon another. As each individual member began listing down different concepts, we connected each topic to list out the similarity and relevance between different topics. Finally, our group and I were able to distinguish and consider three topics that would be important to the project development process.


Mind Map Discoveries

Discovered that important topic to break down and look into may be the topic of psychology , user-friendly feature and functionality of the technology. In addition, app notifications with extreme controllability that could be worn or use another process to alert the user.

Psychology

  • How would user feel about the overall design?
  • Would user still be distracted by the notification system within the VR glasses?
  • How would user react and feel about the VR glass design?
  • What would be an appropriate colour for the interface design?

Usability

  • How would we design the VR glasses so that all users are able to access settings easily?
  • What would be an appropriate colour choice for the interface for all users?
  • Is the VR glass haptic or is there physical button that user has to press?

Functionality

  • What types of functionality would be appropriate for all users?
  • What are the best and appropriate software tools for the development?
  • What kind of resources (from past technologies) can we use for product development?

Low Fidelity Prototyping

Paper Prototype Design


Key Takeaways From Research

From mindmapping, the key points our group has pointed out were psychology, usability and functionality. From the card sorting and idea prioritisation, we believed that while feature functionality, aesthetics and demographics are important factors, nothing is more important than safety, privacy and security of the users.


Before crafting paper prototype, our group discussed about best way to keep users safe from privacy, health and distractions. In addition, our group discussed about best ways to handle the interface with the VR glass. After discussing safety and privacy/security, I brought an appropriate tool for low fidelity model production.

Approach & Design

From the key takeaways, we learned that nothing is more important than the safety, privacy and security of the users when using the product. Therefore, we decided to implemented a user account and login feature for personal profile setting, user setting and privacy preference settings. That way, users may feel more safe with the product when using them in public.

Our team decided to implement a scroll with eyes functionality. Reasoning for eye scroll functionality is because of personal space safety. For instance, if a person is using a public transportation in a crowded space and moves around with hand gestures, then other person may get hit and distract the personal space. Therefore, our team considered personal space safety and decided to implement safe eye scroll functionality for the product.

Key Findings, Results & Issues

Some of the buttons were not functioning correctly and users became lost.

There were too many long descriptions that made users confused.

Users became lost after multiple tries to get to the different page.

Some of the icons did not make sense to the users.

Create folder and application management set up did not exist.

Some of the small buttons lead to a unintended screen/page.

Our team has found out that there needs to be more artboard for interaction flow and current digital interface (High fidelity mockup) needs a lot of revision in making buttons/functionality more clear to the users.
