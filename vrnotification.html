OVERVIEW

About the project

Problem: With the growing number of smartphone users and advancing technologies of wearable devices, there are also growing concern of safety issues. Many people are often distracted by smartphone or wearable technologies' notification systems. This project is focused on examining people's behavior with notification system and redesigning notification management system content with wearable devices such as wearable glasses and watches.

Solution: The solution proposed is a wearable device interface that helps user to manage their notification settings by a level of importance and priorities. Users are able to identify the importance of the notifcation with the different haptic and tone.

Duration: 2 months

My role: As a designer, I was responsible for planning of focus group, organizing and documenting contextual inquiry data, responsible for conducting mind mapping and card sorting session, creating low fidelity mockup designs, creating high fidelity visual design, and conducting usability testing sessions.

CONTEXT

Context: Over the past 3 decades, the way we communicate, interact and respond to daily reminders, messages and tasks have changed drastically. In the 1990s, we responded to our communication by beeping pagers, then it moved onto answering text messages using hand motion/gestures. Since the first introduction of the smartphone, people now read and tap on the flat screen to respond to the notifications and reminders. Technologies are continuing to evolve and there are new technological devices coming up every year with different features/functionality. However, 3154 people have been killed and 424,000 people have been injured in 2013 by smartphone notification distractions while driving. It is now time to reduce the injury/fatality caused by smartphone distraction, and with the new introduction and new practices with VR technology, there needs to be a better way to view personal notification and avoid distractions.

FOCUS GROUP

Qualitative Research

We wanted to closely examine people's opinion, feelings, and attitudes with the current notification management experiences. Currently, there are many technological devices that users use such as iPhone, Android, iPad, MacBook, PC, and Android tablets. In order to identify user's frustrations and needs, I planned and created a focus group session by recruiting 5 participants, assigning roles for each members in the team (moderator, and documentation person) and setting up a video recording device. For personal privacy, our team asked each participants for the recording consent and the session began.

Participant & Session

We chose 5 Indiana University students to the focus group session. All of 4 of our team members with different roles. There were 1 moderator, and 3 documenation person, taking notes on understanding mental models, and documenting participant's frustrations, and past experiences.

Focus Group Questions

In order to identify details on people's experience with notification system and management, we asked questions that were open ended and not simply a yes or no questions. Questions included such as

  1. How often do you find a notification bothering you?
  2. What are the ways that you manage multiple notifications?
  3. What are your opinions on your phone notifications?
  4. How quickly do you take action when you see a notification? (response time)
  5. Describe any situations that made you want to turn off certain notifications?
  6. How do notifications affect the time you spend on your phone?

CARD SORTING

Clarifying user comprehension and categorization

In addition to the focus group, we wanted to see how the users categorize each features on current notification systems and devices. In order to identify user's needs and thought process, we created a card sorting session for users to sort out cards with different notification content features on the card. Similar to the focus group, we assigned a moderator to go over the instructions for the card sorting session. The overall goal was to generate options for features, contents, and information to create a better way to manage notifications.

Key takeaways

After the card sorting session, our team learned that users have categorized list of features by level of importance and priority levels. Cards are categorized by one, two, and three stars with one being least important to three being the most important. The three star notes were mostly features that are related to privacy, psychology, and security impact. Two star notes were mainly functionality features and convenience. One star notes (least important) were mainly features that are less impactful to users (i.e GPS notifications, gaming notifications, and etc).

MIND MAPPING

Research insights & synthesis

According to our research from focus groups and card sorting, our team took away and learned that psychology, security, and privacy is a very important factor in notification management settings. In order to connect between the research result and the concept generation, we created a mind map to identify our central theme. Our goal was to visually come to a solution to the problem space to better generate solution to the issue.

Mind map discoveries

The central theme of this project was notification. From the central theme, all of our team members began listing important topics that would be helpful to solve notification management issues. Our team listed down topics such as physical impact, setting functionality impact, haptic functionality, filtering feature, priority settings, and security impact. Furthermore, our team listed down possible approaches to each topic to help solve user's frustration and needs.

STORYBOARDING

Identifying different narratives

The goal for the storyboarding was to use the idea that we generated from mind mapping and research and create visual narratives to understand scenarios that users can run into. After the storyboarding, our team was finally able to decide and which design decisions to make and functionality to develop for better notification management for the users.

CONCEPT GENERATION

Low fidelity prototyping

Before crafting low fidelity prototype, our team discussed about ideas to create a product to keep users safe from privacy invasion, health impact and distractions. As a result, our team discussed and made a decision to create an interface for the wearable devices such as smart watch and virtual reality glasses.

VISUAL DESIGN

High fidelity prototyping

After spending time reflecting upon research insights, and analysis, our team spent time making design decisions and creating functionality constraints for the wearable device interface. With the important emphasis on personal psychology, security, and, privacy, our team spent began implementing key features and funtionalities to the wearable device interface using Figma.

1. Secure & Convenient User Login System

In order to have users login to the notification management interface system without a hassle, we implemented a Facebook and Gmail API login feature.

2. Easy Navigation to Notification Management

In order to manage notifications quicker and convenient, the interface is designed with only 4 major functionalities that categorizes the needs (notification page, safety settings, priority management, and history). Users are able to access high, medium, and low priority page and manage their notification preferences.

3. Easy Edit, Manage Creatively

Users are able to click into each applications for detailed notification modification. Users can simply drag and drop each notification topic to high, medium, and low priority pages to mark the importance of the notifications. High, medium, and low priority notifications can be notified with different haptic features and tones.

4. Safety first, History and Missed Notification in User's Convenient Time

One of the main goal was to keep users safe from notification distraction. To solve these issues, users can edit safety mode and view notifications later in user's convenient time. Users can also view history to see their past decisions and response.

USABILITY TESTING

Wizard of Oz testing

After the high fidelity visual design prototype, we wanted to test and see how users feel about our product. The Wizard of Oz testing was conducted at Indiana University library with 3 participants. Prototype was tested by Figma prototype presentation model and users were given a direction to think aloud as they navigate through different parts of the interface.

Key Findings

Overall, all 3 testers gave a positive feedbacks of our prototype. None of the people faced troubles navigating through different parts of the interface and expressed conveniences in managing their notifications. In terms of safety, privacy, and psychologial aspect of the interface, all testers has expressed positive feedbacks on those issues and believed that the prototype was safe.
