User Interviews

In order to understand user's experience with the luxury vehicles and their interest, we conducted an interview to 10 different people from all different demographics. The Interview was conducted in different locations around Indiana University campus and interviewers were aged from 19 - 70 with mixed gender. The interviews took about 10 minutes each and as the interview progressed, each of our team member documented people's insights and experience.

Interview Questions

Do you prefer selecting luxury cars when you rent a car?

How do you usually rent a car?

Do you ever use mobile app to book a car, if so which one and how is your experience?

How satisfied with the services offered by the current car booking application?

Tell me about your experience with the cars you booked. What were the pros and cons?

How was the car condition like when you booked and used the car?

Tell me about any negative experiences you had with car reservations if you have any.

Name: Alvaro

Use Cases & Features List

Use Cases Breakdown (Right Image)

After analyzing the research data and feedback our team collected, our team designed the use cases for this application. There are 4 user classes that our team designed: Clients(Customers), Drivers, Car rental companies, and for all app users. Each user classes are outlined below.

All Users: In order for all users to maintain convenient profile management and to maintain privacy, log in/out design and functionality is one of the top use cases. For user's convenience, username and password reset is another important factor to the use cases.

Clients: Main user of our application. Managing car rentals, being able to cancel/change reservation, price ranges, location, date ranges and reservation history are a really important use cases for the customers. Based on the research data, accessibility (location, date, and price ranges) were the biggest issue and challenge from the car rental users.

Drivers: User profile and cases that determines eligibility. Checking for driver insurance, agreeing to rental agreements, and customer background check is another important factor for this application use cases

Rental Companies: This user case focuses on car inventories, ownership and car lending notification.

Objectives

VICI Transport's mission is to allow ground transportation companies worldwide to have clients book reservation, static displays, and chauffeur services all through a mobile application.

Feature List Breakdown (Left Image)

What are the key things that user need in this application? What elements in this app would be successful? and least successful?

High Priority Features: features such as the login/logout, date ranges, available cities, payment accessibility, insurance, and agreement form.

Why it's important: Based on the research, people value personal privacy, safety, and accessibility the most. Without focusing on these elements, users may not be satisfied and trust this application.

Medium & Low Priorities: description tab, rental history, personal settings, push notification feature, and ratings and reviews.

Why it's less important: Based on research, features such as rental history, push notification settings, reviews, and description does not invade people's privacy and does not give a big impact or affect to people's daily life.

High Fidelity Model & Design

Changes after Usability Testing

Our team wanted to make sure to supply an application that would be user-friendly along with having that ease of use feature. In doing so, we realized some changes needed to be made in order to seamlessly transition from our low fidelity into our high fidelity mockup. During our user testing, our team ran into some problems that need to be changed in order to make a better user experience. Our team got rid of some screens, created additional screens, and changed some existing screens The biggest changes included getting rid of the organize client information screen and in-app messaging. When our participants were doing their user testing they didn’t understand why there would need to be functionality and communication between the vehicle rental company and them. As a team, we decided to get rid of functionality for the car rental companies and communication with them. Instead, we are offering our clients a different way that they can contact the company if need be. Our team decided to place an information tab on each vehicle posting that will include the rental company’s name, address, phone number, and email. This will allow the user to call, text, or email the company with any questions or concerns that users may have. Below shows images of sample high fidelity mockup design and screens. Programming & Implementation

Changes from High Fidelity Prototype

From our very first high fidelity prototype experience using Adobe XD, our team received a feedback from variety of people including participants from low fidelity prototype/testing. Users have pointed out that all functionality within the app was very well displayed and conveniently accessible to all users. In terms of design, our team has received couple of points of improvement. First was the consistent color scheme and color choice practices. The pink background with white color typography and fonts made some color blind users hard to see. Moreover, some users pointed out that the pink background with white colored font made the application too flashy. Another major feedback was consistent color theme/design practices. Some users pointed out that in the UI Buttons below had too many different colors and design practices were not consistent. Instead, some users recommended choosing 2-3 colors at maximum and utilizing those 2-3 color as a background, typography, and for other designs. Another major change between the high fidelity prototype and implementation phase was the usage of login/sign up system. The high fidelity model of our application displayed manual login/sign up using username and password formation. Our manager has pointed out that login/sign up data set up can take a long time, and implementing Facebook or Gmail login instead would save up a lot of time in development process. Moreover, our manager (potential user) has pointed out the convenient Facebook setting management system where users can easily manage credential settings through other media platforms (i.e resetting password through Facebook and etc). With those 2 major feedback in mind, our team moved onto the programming implementation phase.

Result of the Application

Throughout the programming and implementation process, our team considered the consistent design style and practices by keeping only few colors as a background and typography. Moreover, we wanted to make sure our font and description outlined in the application is legible to all users. Along with the consistent design and typography practices, our team also took consideration of conveniences within navigating through different parts of the application. For user's convenience, our team has successfully implemented the Facebook login process for users to login and manage their account. Furthermore, all payments done through this application is done through PayPal account, where users can simply enter their debit card, or PayPal information for future purchases.

Another most important feature our team implemented in this application is the selection by dates, location, and price range option. From our research, our team learned that accessibility and affordability is a major key issue that users go through. In order to help users to access luxury vehicle at their convenient location, dates, and price range, our team implemented the location filter, date filter and price range filter functionality to help users access the vehicle.

Overall, the Research, Design, Product Management and Software Development process took 8 months of work between the team of 4 people. Our team accomplished the goal of mastering the XCode skills, Adobe Creative Suit skills, and product management skills using Agile.

Reflection on the Project

The VICI-Transport mobile application development project was the longest, most challenging, and most rewarding project I have ever done. Throughout this project, I learned to become a great collaborator, great compromiser, and a great problem solver. Prior to this project, I never had any experience in any prototype tools, product management using Agile, software development using XCode/Swift, low fidelity design and usability testing. In the very beginning, I felt excited, and nervous at the same time. However, I strongly believed that this project and this experience would help me prepare for my future career, and shape who I am as a UX Designer in the future.

There were many ups and downs through out this project. Some of our team mate faced major challenges in software development, which made them lose interest to continue on this project. As a result, our manager started off by scolding at our team, and stating that we are not going to be able to accomplish this project. That is when our team learned the lesson, and decided not to give up. Every Wednesday and Sunday, our team gathered together and started watching Youtube tutorials on Swift basics, Agile basics, and data structure basics. Whenever we were stuck and faced a difficult time configuring our code or designing our application, we reached out to one another and helped one another. Every week, we started understanding the XCode basics, and process, which lead to completing more lines of code, and compiling. Even after we started picking up on basic concept of Swift, and other software skills, our professor and manager kept telling us that "you are behind from other teams". However we didn't quit.

Starting from the research process, our team wanted to deeply focus on the users and understand what users think and feel of the existing technology. While some of our team members got started on the user research, I started investing my time into reviewing any technical challenges, and prepared for any technical challenges so I could help rest of our team members. First, I downloaded the XCode, Command terminal, and got familiarized with Git basic functionalities through Youtube tutorial and helpful books. After downloading all the hardware on my computer, I helped downloading all these hardware to our teammates' computer. As a result, our team saved up a lot of time by helping each other from the start. After downloading and familiarizing all the hardware tools and software tools, our team finished all the research and began analyzing all the data using all the research methods. For many members in our team, they were new to the whole UX research process. With the past experience I had with UX research and design, I began assisting others by reviewing some of the UX research method and by teaching them some of the basic process of UX.

Moving onto the design phase, which was on the 16th week of this project, many of our team members were new to the low fidelity wireframing and high fidelity mockup software tools like Adobe XD. We did a lot of practice runs so that all of our team members can get used to and become familiarize with the low fidelity and high fidelity mockup design practices. After couple of practices, all of our team members were able to come up with mockup designs and showcase our high fidelity design to our manager and our users (usability testing participants). As a result of collaborating, communicating, and assisting others who are in need, our team received an A+ in our design projects and assignments.

Our team entered the programming and implementation phase on January 2020. Over the 2 weeks winter break period, our team spent time learning and familiarizing XCode basics. Overall, this project was long, challenging but rewarding project.
