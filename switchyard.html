OVERVIEW

About the project

The goal and objective of this project was to identify different types of outdoor activities, outdoor activity location preference, understand people's motivation on involvement with outdoor activities, and generate visual concept ideas by incorporating AR technologies. As a designer, I wanted to understand people's motivation on adventuring outside, and what they want to accomplish while spending their time outdoors. This quantitative research lead to a wireframing and prototyping of mobile AR interactive gaming app.

My role

User Research, Site Research, Visual Design, Concept Generation, and Prototyping

Duration

2 months

THE CHALLENGE

Design and develop a mobile app that can motivate people for all age to adventure outdoors, and help people to get motivated to exercise outdoors more often

According to WHO research studies, the study shows that more than 80% of global teens don't exercise for at least one hour. This may lead to both physical and mental illness in the long term. Mixing game and outdoor involvement can help motivate people to exercise or adventure outdoors more often.

SITE RESEARCH

Challenges in understanding location and space

This project started off with the site research. Initially, our team was assigned with the one location in Bloomington, Indiana for site research. The site that our team was assigned to research was Switchyard Park. Switchyard Park is a 65.29 acre park located in South Eastern suburb of Bloomingoton, Indiana. For this research, our team began the site research by searching park's history, amenities, construction timeline, accessibility, and services that they offer.

Key Findings

From the initial site research, we collected information on park's history, amenities, accessibility, and services that they offer. I learned that the park is relatively new, offers a lots of services, amenities, and events for people in all ages. Furthermore, I learned that this park is accessible by car, and bicycles with parking lot and roads/trails. This site research information helped us in our project process in generating concept ideas and understanding meaning & form of the park.

PHOTOETHNOGRAPHY

Capturing visual elements & observation

To closely observe visual elements of the amenities, services, and accessibility offered at the Switchyard park, our team went to the Switchyard park, and captured photos. Each team member visited different facility (playground, landmark, trail, and etc), and location of the park and took the photos. Toward the end, our team combined all the photos that we took and choose the photos that best displays Switchyard park.

CONCEPT GENERATION

Idea generation after gathering research

After gathering site research data and capturing all the visual details of the park, our team started brainstorming and sketching some ideas to help users to engage outdoor activities at Switchyard park. Using Adobe Capture, paper, and pen, each member drew preliminary sketches on ideas such as the web interface, QR code functionality ideas, and user engaging visual elements such as the historical elements and gaming.

Key Takeaways

All of our team members sketched out our ideas and features for the product that can help to motivate users to adventure outdoors more by incorporating the key findings from Switchyard park research. We drew a tiny URL interface that can help generate information about the park, QR code readers that can help users to scan and learn more about the park on the website, and gaming ideas with mission/quest. Now that we generated all the creative initial sketches and concepts, we conducted a meaning & form synthesis to create visual elements, and aggrevate all our visual ideas for the potential product.

VISUAL DESIGN

Prototyping & proposal

After conducting all the researches, our team decided to design a outdoor mobile app by incorporating Augemented Reality (AR) technology and the live gaming. For this design choice, we decided to incorporate historical elements where users can perform and accomplish certain mission and earn scores. Users can select NPC character of their choice, find a quest/mission to take and accomplish, earn points as they finish their quest. The overall goal for this design choice was to bring the entertainment, joy, and courage to the people who are looking to have fun while spending their time outdoors. Although this application design was heavily influenced by the research of Switchyard park, this game can be played anywhere in any location.

Landing page, and select avatar (character) screen

To help users to explore and be engaged with this application, I choose 3 colors for the color palette with natural themed color. Users can click get started and get to select their avatar of their choice. Users can unlock their avatar by using some of the coins user has earned. There are 2 free avatar that users can choose from, view details and choose.

Simplistic navigation, AR object, and live gaming

In order to for users to navigate through different resources such as the map, quest/mission list, and rewards option, I designed a swipe menu on the bottom for users to swipe and select. Similar concept to Pokemon Go, NPC character will pop up for interaction and involvement. Camera takes up most of the interface screen for the safety concern. (having small camera screen interface might make users hard to see). Once users click hide, menu will be shrinked and there will be a full camera screen.

Maps, reward shops, and pickup

Using the Google Maps API, the map feature shows the location of the user. The Map will also how where the quest/mission (indicated with treasure box) is located with indication of streets, parks, and buildings. Once users complete certain tasks and makes accomplishments, users will earn points (indicated on the top right corner on every page). With the coins that users earn, users will be able to purchase rewards using the coins they earned. Users can select and pick certain item, view details, and either make a purchase at the store or get their items delivered (delivery feature was implemented after the spread of COVID-19)

REFLECTION

Key learnings & takeaways

From this project, I took away many important lessons on UX research and design concepts. This project was done part of a class course project, and was based on the University's curriculum. However, the curriculum helped me learn important steps of UX processes. Usually in the first step of UX projects, I previously conducted a user research by directly reaching out to people with user surveys, and interviews. From this project, I took away another method and ways to understand user's behavior and goals by conducting the site/location research. Moreover, I was introduced to new research methods such as meaning and form exploration where we get to dive into the visual images and get to explore different aspects of design.

This was my first time being introduced to the AR concept design in mobile app project. With my strong interest in PokemonGo, I was always interested in designing a live AR game with adventurous missions in it. As a result, I utilized Photoshop to carefully edit the NPC character and implemented the NPC in the mobile interface. Furthermore, with my previous experiences with Google Maps API, I designed a map feature for this application for users to see. After working with the NPC photoshop and analyzing the collection of photo from the photoethnography, I began prototyping the entire application using Figma. Overall, I took away some great lessons and tips from UX research to design phase, and I strongly believed that this experience will help me understand user's goal, and help me to better process solution for the users.
